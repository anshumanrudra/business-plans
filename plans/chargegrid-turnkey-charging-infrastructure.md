## Executive Summary

**The Opportunity:**  
EV charging infrastructure is expanding globally, but property owners, municipalities, and businesses struggle to navigate the complexity of deploying stations—site assessment, equipment selection, electrical design, contractor coordination, regulatory compliance, and operations setup. Most either pay premium prices to large operators (ChargePoint, EVgo) who own and operate stations, or work with local electricians who lack EV-specific expertise.

**The Solution:**  
ChargeGrid Solutions delivers profitable EV charging stations from concept to cash flow. We provide end-to-end turnkey services: feasibility studies, engineering design, equipment procurement, installation oversight, and operations setup. Property owners keep ownership, we take 20-30% project margin + optional 5-10% ongoing revenue share for long-term partnership.

**Why Now:**  
- Global charging infrastructure investment: $50B+ through 2030
- Governments mandating station deployment (US NEVI, EU Green Deal)
- Property owners want charging amenities but lack expertise
- Market fragmented—no dominant turnkey solutions provider for SMB segment

**Business Model:**  
Project-based fees ($3K-25K per station) for immediate revenue + optional ongoing revenue share for recurring income. Target: 10-12 projects in Year 1 = $90K-135K total revenue, profitable from Month 6.

## 3. Business Model & Revenue Streams

### Revenue Model Breakdown

**Primary Revenue: Project Fees (75% of Year 1 revenue)**

**How it works:**
- Client pays 30-50% deposit upfront (covers equipment procurement)
- 30-40% milestone payment (upon installation completion)
- 20-30% final payment (upon successful commissioning)

**Your costs:**
- Equipment (pass-through, mark up 20-30%)
- Contractor labor (pass-through, mark up 10-15%)
- Your time (project management, design, oversight)
- Travel & expenses (typically 5-10% of project budget)

**Gross margin:** 20-30% on total project cost

**Example (Commercial Hub - $40K project):**
- Equipment cost to you: $18K (sell to client for $23K) → $5K margin
- Contractor cost to you: $10K (sell to client for $11.5K) → $1.5K margin
- Your project management fee: $5.5K
- **Total your revenue:** $12K
- **Your costs:** $1K (travel, tools, CAD designer) 
- **Net profit:** $11K (27.5% net margin)

---

**Secondary Revenue: Revenue Share (25% of Year 1, growing to 40%+ by Year 3)**

**How it works:**
- 5-10% of monthly gross charging revenue
- Contract: 3-5 years with annual renewal option
- Payment: Monthly via automated revenue report from charging software

**Example (Commercial Hub with 1 DC + 2 AC):**
- Monthly charging revenue: $3,000-5,000 (depends on utilization)
- Your 8% share: $240-400/month = $2,880-4,800/year
- Over 3 years: $8,640-14,400 (nearly equals original project fee!)

**Why clients accept this:**
- Ongoing expert support without retainer
- Aligned incentives (you optimize their revenue)
- Flexibility (they can buy out after Year 1 if they prefer)

**Scalability:**
- 10 stations deployed in Year 1 → $25K-50K/year recurring by Year 2
- 30 stations by Year 3 → $75K-150K/year recurring (passive income)

## 7. Key Risks & Mitigation

### Critical Risks

**1. Project Delays (HIGH PROBABILITY)**

**Risk:** Permitting, utility connections, or contractor delays push timelines 30-50% longer.

**Impact:** Cash flow delays (final payment upon completion), client dissatisfaction, negative reviews.

**Mitigation:**
- Buffer 30% extra time in all project timelines (under-promise, over-deliver)
- Apply for permits immediately upon contract signing (don't wait)
- Build relationships with utility companies (expedite connections)
- Have backup contractors (if primary flakes, switch quickly)
- Milestone payments (don't wait for full completion to get paid)

---

**2. Customer Payment Issues (MEDIUM-HIGH PROBABILITY)**

**Risk:** Clients delay payments, negotiate down, or dispute invoices.

**Impact:** Cash flow crunch, can't pay vendors, project stops.

**Mitigation:**
- 30-50% deposit BEFORE ordering equipment (non-negotiable)
- Milestone payments tied to deliverables, not time (30% on install, 20% on commissioning)
- Use contracts (clear payment terms, late fees, escalation clause)
- For large projects, use escrow (client deposits funds, released upon milestones)
- Credit check commercial clients (D&B reports)
- Walk away from clients with payment history red flags

---

**3. Contractor Quality Issues (MEDIUM PROBABILITY)**

**Risk:** Poor workmanship, safety violations, delays, or contractor abandoning project.

**Impact:** Client dissatisfaction, warranty claims, your reputation damaged, cost overruns.

**Mitigation:**
- Vet contractors thoroughly (licenses, insurance, references, past work)
- Site inspections at critical milestones (foundation, electrical rough-in, final install)
- Hold back 10-20% payment until final sign-off
- Require insurance certificates (general liability, workers comp)
- Have backup contractors identified
- Document everything (photos, inspection reports)

---

**4. Low Margins / Price Competition (MEDIUM PROBABILITY)**

**Risk:** Competitors undercut pricing, clients push back on quotes, margins compressed to 10-15%.

**Impact:** Unprofitable projects, can't scale, burnout from low-value work.

**Mitigation:**
- Compete on value, not price (quality, speed, ongoing support)
- Focus on underserved markets (small cities, niche properties)
- Standardize to reduce costs (reusable designs, vendor discounts for volume)
- Walk away from unprofitable projects (not worth the effort)
- Upsell revenue share (immediate fee lower but long-term return higher)

---

**5. International Expansion Complexity (MEDIUM PROBABILITY)**

**Risk:** Regulatory differences, contractor quality varies, travel costs high, cultural/language barriers.

**Impact:** Projects fail, reputation suffers in new markets, capital wasted.

**Mitigation:**
- Start with English-speaking markets (easier communication)
- Partner with local consultants for first project in each country (learn regulations)
- Hire local contractors (you design remotely, they execute, you inspect key milestones)
- Research regulations thoroughly before bidding (electrical codes, permits, incentives)
- Start with one international project (pilot), then scale if successful

---

**6. Technology Obsolescence (LOW PROBABILITY)**

**Risk:** Charging standards change (CCS → new standard), equipment you recommended becomes outdated.

**Impact:** Client dissatisfaction, warranty claims, reputation damage.

**Mitigation:**
- Stay vendor-agnostic (recommend equipment based on today's standards, not loyalty)
- Recommend future-proof equipment (CCS, OCPP 2.0, upgradeable firmware)
- Educate clients (EV tech evolves, but backwards compatibility typical)
- Offer upgrade services (revenue opportunity, not threat)
- Join industry associations (get early signals on standards changes)
