## Detailed Business Plan: EV Charging Station under PM E-Drive Scheme (India)

### 1. Understanding the PM E-Drive Scheme (launched Oct 2024)

- Successor to the FAME-II scheme, with a total outlay of ₹10,900 crore (2024-2026 window, likely extended).
- A dedicated ₹2,000 crore allocation exists for EV charging infrastructure.
- Subsidies cover a portion of capital cost for setting up public charging stations.
- The scheme is administered by the Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI) and implemented via agencies like BEEE (Bureau of Energy Efficiency).
- Charger specifications must comply with MHI/BIS standards.

### 2. Business Registration & Legal Setup

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| Business entity | Register a Private Limited Company or LLP (LLP is simpler and cheaper for a first-time owner). An OPC (One Person Company) also works. |
| GST Registration | Mandatory — EV charging is classified as a "service" (GST rate: 18% currently, but check latest). |
| Trade License | From local municipal corporation. |
| MSME Registration (Udyam) | Register on udyamregistration.gov.in — unlocks additional subsidies and easier bank loans. |
| Electrical License | From the State Electrical Inspectorate for HT/LT connections. |
| NOC | Fire department NOC if using DC fast chargers. |

### 3. Site Selection (Critical)

Best locations to evaluate:
- Near commercial hubs, malls, office complexes
- Petrol pump premises (many are adding EV chargers)
- Highway corridors (every 25 km as per MoP guidelines)
- Near residential societies with high EV adoption
- Bus depots / fleet operator hubs

Key criteria:
- Availability of adequate power supply (minimum 3-phase connection)
- Visibility and easy vehicle access
- Proximity to existing electrical infrastructure (transformer, substation)
- Minimum 200-400 sq ft for a small station, more for multi-charger setups

### 4. Charging Infrastructure — What to Install

| Type | Power | Use Case | Cost per unit (approx) | Charging Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AC Slow Charger (3.3-7.2 kW) | Low | 2-wheelers, overnight 4W | ₹50K - ₹1.5L | 4-8 hours |
| AC Fast Charger (22 kW) | Medium | 4-wheelers | ₹2L - ₹4L | 1-2 hours |
| DC Fast Charger (30-60 kW) | High | 4-wheelers, commercial | ₹8L - ₹20L | 30-60 min |
| DC Rapid Charger (120+ kW) | Very High | Highway, fleet | ₹25L - ₹50L+ | 15-30 min |

Recommended starting config for a mixed station:
- 2x AC slow chargers (for 2-wheelers)
- 1-2x DC fast chargers (30-60 kW for 4-wheelers)
- Total equipment cost: ₹15L - ₹35L depending on specs

### 5. Applying Under PM E-Drive

1. Monitor the MHI portal (mhi.gov.in) and BEEE portal for Expression of Interest (EOI) / tender notifications.
2. Register as a Charge Point Operator (CPO) on the portal.
3. Submit application with:
    - Business registration documents
    - Proposed site details with photos and coordinates
    - Technical specifications of chargers (must be BIS-certified)
    - Power availability proof from DISCOM
4. Upon approval, install chargers within the stipulated timeline (usually 6-12 months).
5. Subsidy is typically disbursed after installation, inspection, and commissioning.

### 6. Power Supply / DISCOM Connection

- Apply to local DISCOM for a dedicated EV charging connection.
- Many states offer a special EV tariff (₹4-6/kWh vs ₹8-10 commercial rate) — check your state.
- For DC fast chargers, you'll likely need an HT (High Tension) connection — plan for this early as it can take 2-4 months.
- Consider solar panels (rooftop) to offset electricity costs — additional subsidies available under PM Surya Ghar.

| Net investment | ₹15L - ₹40L |

### 8. Revenue Model

- Per-unit charging fee: ₹12-18/kWh (market rate) against a cost of ₹5-8/kWh
- Gross margin: ~50-60% per kWh
- Additional revenue: parking fees, convenience store, advertising on digital screens at station
- Typical breakeven: 18-36 months depending on utilization
- Target: 8-15 charging sessions/day to be viable

### 9. Technology & Software

- Use OCPP (Open Charge Point Protocol) compliant chargers — this is mandatory for interoperability.
- Partner with a Charging Management System (CMS) provider or use platforms like Statiq, Kazam, ElectricPe as aggregators.
- Mobile app integration for payments (UPI, cards) is essential.
- Remote monitoring and diagnostics to minimize downtime.

### 11. Risks & Mitigation

| Technology obsolescence | Use modular, upgradeable chargers |
| Power outages | Battery backup / solar hybrid setup |

| Competition from Tata Power, Adani | Focus on underserved locations; offer better service |

### 13. First-Time Business Owner Tips

- Don't over-invest on day one. Start with 1 station, learn the operations, then scale.
- His pharma/drug discovery analytical skills are actually transferable — site selection, ROI analysis, regulatory navigation are similar muscles.
- Join EV charging WhatsApp/Telegram groups and attend EV expos (like EV India Expo, Auto Expo) for networking.
- Hire a local electrician with HT/LT experience as a part-time consultant.
- Keep 6 months of operating expenses as reserve.
- Maintain meticulous records from day one — it helps with subsidy claims and future bank loans.

## Reality Check: ₹5L Budget vs. DC Fast Charging

DC fast chargers alone cost ₹8L-₹20L+ per unit. With civil work, electrical infrastructure, leasing, and permits, a single DC fast charging station realistically costs ₹25L-₹50L minimum. So ₹5L personal capital
is a starting point, not the full picture. Here's how to make it work:

Funding Strategy to Bridge the Gap

| Source | Amount (approx) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personal capital | ₹5L | Seed money for registration, initial costs |
| Partner(s) | ₹10L-₹20L | Find 1-2 partners (see below) |
| Bank loan (MSME/Mudra) | ₹10L-₹15L | Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (up to ₹10L under Tarun category) or MSME term loan |
| PM E-Drive subsidy | ₹5L-₹15L | Reimbursed post-installation |
| State (Karnataka) EV subsidy | Additional possible | Karnataka EV Policy 2023-2028 has provisions |
| Total accessible | ₹30L-₹55L | Enough for a solid single-station setup |

The subsidy comes after installation, so he needs upfront capital from partners + loan to cover the gap.

### Finding the Right Partner

Since he's looking for a partner, here are the best approaches:

1. Franchise / Brand Partner model — Companies like Statiq, Kazam, Tata Power EZ Charge, and Exicom sometimes offer partnership models where they provide equipment at reduced cost or on revenue-share, and you
   provide the location + operations. This dramatically reduces upfront capital. Your brother should directly contact:
    - **Statiq** — statiq.in/partner
    - **Tata Power EZ Charge** — they have a franchise model
    - **Kazam EV** — kazam.in
    - **Exicom** — they manufacture chargers and also do partnerships

2. Financial partner — Someone who puts in ₹15L-₹20L for an equity stake (say 40-50%). Look within his professional network, local business groups, or Bengaluru startup meetups.

3. Property owner as partner — Find a commercial property owner willing to provide space rent-free or at reduced rent in exchange for a revenue share. This is increasingly common — property owners see EV charging
   as a footfall driver.

Option 1 (franchise/brand partner) is the strongest path for a first-time owner with limited capital. He gets brand recognition, technical support, customer discovery via their app, and lower capex.

### Honest Advice for a First-Time Owner

1. Don't rush to quit the pharma job. The charging station won't generate meaningful income for 6-12 months. Keep the salary coming.
2. The franchise/brand partner route is the smartest move at ₹5L personal capital. It de-risks almost everything — technology, customer acquisition, maintenance.
3. Location is 80% of the game. Spend more time on site selection than anything else. Visit potential spots at different times of day, count EV traffic, talk to nearby shop owners.
4. BESCOM connection is the biggest bottleneck. Apply early, follow up weekly, consider hiring a local electrical contractor who has BESCOM relationships.
5. Start with a strong 2-wheeler play. Bengaluru has massive Ather/Ola/TVS iQube penetration. Fleet delivery riders are desperate for fast charging. This can be his bread-and-butter while 4-wheeler volume builds.
6. Keep meticulous books from day one. Use Zoho Books or Khatabook. Clean financials make subsidy claims and future loans much easier.
